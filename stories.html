Strategic intake and backlog planning

Shifting feature delivery from reactive execution to intentional product planning.

Situation

The organization did not have a structured project management process, which caused operations to lean reactive. Features were often created after an issue had already surfaced or after a market shift had already happened.

Action

I initiated a culture shift by creating recurring alignment between executives and managers, connecting business goals to a product backlog, and establishing an intake process so every new task was not automatically treated as high priority.

Impact

This creates a path from reactive work to intentional planning, reduces waste, and lowers the risk of rework caused by multitasking, unclear priorities, and shifting expectations during sprint execution.

Process improvement

Improving review flow for an international team through clearer acceptance criteria.

Situation

I assessed waste and delays in our delivery process and found that review timing was a major source of slowdown for an international team. Work could wait at least a full workday before review began, and when corrections were needed, the feedback cycle could delay development by at least two days.

Action

I helped create a delivery environment where tasks were described in more detail, including required QA checks and specific acceptance criteria. I also encouraged project team members to raise questions during sprint kickoff so unclear requirements could be clarified, improved, and documented before work began.

Impact

This reduced ambiguity before execution, lowered the risk of rework during review, and supported smoother asynchronous collaboration. It also helped shift the team toward a stronger shared understanding of what done meant before development started.

AI tool change management

Helping developers integrate AI tools without losing engineering ownership.

Situation

Developers were expected to integrate AI tools into their work to improve efficiency, but usage was still rare. The team needed a practical adoption path that addressed tool choice, quality concerns, and worries that AI could reduce engineering creativity.

Action

I kept stakeholders closely involved from the start. We compared the pros and cons of each AI tool, ran controlled stress tests on shortlisted options, aligned on a default tool, discussed current usage habits, and created work agreements and documentation guidelines with the developers.

Impact

The process gave the team shared evaluation criteria, stronger adoption buy-in, and clearer practices for using AI transparently. The documentation guidelines also reduced the black-box effect of AI-generated models and supported cross-team collaboration.

AI operations adoption

Moving a 1,440-hour data collection process into an AI-enabled workflow.

Situation

A high-volume ESG data collection workflow required too much manual effort, making delivery speed, cost, and consistency difficult to sustain.

Action

I initiated an AI-driven program, coordinated workflow redesign, supported development team adoption, clarified ownership, and built change-management routines around the new process.

Result

Processing time dropped from 1,440 hours to 180 hours, accelerating operations 8x and reducing estimated hourly manpower cost by 87.5%.

Technical project enablement

Helping resolve a circular dependency challenge in an imputation pipeline.

Situation

The team was improving an imputation pipeline that estimates missing values using company-level trends, or industry-level trends when company history is limited. The pipeline was performing well, but some related indicators needed to reconcile with one another, and the first improvement approach ran into circular dependency issues.

Action

Because I had taken time to understand the pipeline, its upstream and downstream processes, and the business reason behind the work, I was able to ask more targeted questions and suggest a methodology that bypassed the circular dependency problem while still meeting the business requirement.

Impact

The work reinforced the value of project managers staying close to technical context. By understanding systems, dependencies, and constraints, I could help identify hidden blockers, connect related processes, and support the team when the path forward was unclear.

Functional team support

Reducing QA friction by turning a manual data-filtering task into a reusable workflow.

Situation

The QA team needed to work with results from an anomaly detection program, but only required a specific subset of the data. They could have filtered it manually, but that would have taken hours and slowed down their work.

Action

Because I understood the team's needs and knew Python could produce the same output in minutes, I prepared the subset for them. I also created a reusable Google Colab version of the code so the team could run the process again without depending on one person.

Impact

The support reduced manual effort, helped QA move faster, and strengthened trust with the functional team. It also turned a one-time assist into a repeatable process improvement that can save hours when the same need comes up again.

Product data accuracy

Turning user-reported data flags into a structured quality improvement project.

Situation

Product users were flagging inaccurate data, and the team needed a clearer way to investigate root causes, prioritize fixes, and measure whether quality was improving.

Action

I initiated a data-accuracy project, created a structured improvement path, coordinated across stakeholders, and connected bug tracking, documentation, and QA workflows to the delivery plan.

Result

With 45% of the work completed, inaccurate data flags fell by at least 89%, from a 2.97% baseline error rate to about 0.33%.

Program operations

Creating repeatable delivery systems for a growing education program.

Situation

A growing school program needed consistent curriculum delivery, reliable parent communication, smooth events, and stronger team operating rhythms.

Action

I led a 15+ person team, established SOPs and QA rubrics, built coaching and scheduling cadences, and planned major events with detailed run-of-show plans and contingency buffers.

Result

The program delivered 42+ major events on time, maintained at least 90% trial-to-enrollment conversion, and strengthened stakeholder satisfaction.

How I Work

A project-management style built for clarity and resilience.

Structure ambiguity

Translate broad goals into scope, ownership, dependencies, decision points, and delivery milestones.

Align the people closest to the work

Create recurring stakeholder rhythms so risks and tradeoffs surface before they become blockers.

Make quality visible

Use documentation, bug tracking, QA routines, and metrics to turn improvement work into a shared system.
